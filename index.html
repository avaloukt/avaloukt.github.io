SoundEScape

A web app for a tagging game.

Summary

SoundEScape was a prototype of a human computation game aiming to engage citizens and visitors with places of interest in Edinburgh via sound tagging.

Responsibilities

  • Initial ideation, research and conceptualisation. The initial idea and motivation for this web app was to intrigue citizens or visitors to explore places that other people find interesting, via sharing personal impressions of a place. In order to ground better my idea, I explored other applications attaching different types of content to places and also the way this was presented to users. The social character of the app soon became clear and the notions of gamification and tagging games arouse to be further researched.

  • User centered game design and formative evaluation. After some research on gamified applications and in specific gamified tagging applications, the chosen game concept was a "treasure hunt" game in which one player would tag a place with sounds in order for the other player to identify the place via its acoustic character. This game concept was developed to low fidelity prototypes, and the different variations of the game strategy where tested with users through gameplay testing and evaluation workshops.

  • Digital prototype and data collection. The final prototype was decided to be developed as a responsive web app so that it would be accesible from mobile and desktop devices and also independent of platform. The user interface and the server side of the app were designed and implemented from scratch to fit the specification of the UX research. In the short period of the deployment of the prototype, a representative dataset of sound tagged places was created for Edinburgh.

Technologies used

  • Back-end: Laravel MVC PHP framework, MySQL
